We love music

The largest Ankylosaurus specimen ever found was 20.5 feet (6.25 meters) long, 5.6 feet (1.7 m) tall at the hips and 4.9 feet (1.5 m) wide, according to the study, published in the Canadian Journal of Earth Sciences.
It likely weighed up to 4 tons (3.6 metric tons).
The species name means "destroyer of shins" in Latin, referring to the ankylosaur's 10-foot-long (3 meters) tail, which was tipped with a club that was likely used to swipe at the legs of predators.
The 75-million-year-old remains of this beast were uncovered in 2016 in Montana's Judith River Formation.
Despite its ferocious appearance — several rows of bony spikes covered its 20-foot-long (6 m) body.
